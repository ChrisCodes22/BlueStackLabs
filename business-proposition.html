02 — The Problem

Where Time Disappears

Idle time on the production floor comes from many sources. Story documentation is one of the most overlooked — it happens constantly, on every job, and it never shows up as a line item until you measure it.

Where Production Technician Time Goes

Approximate breakdown of non-wrench time per shift

Waiting for parts / approvals 35%
Walking / staging / setup 22%
Manual story / documentation writing 18%
Team communication / handoffs 15%
Other admin tasks 10%

Story documentation (18%) is the only idle time category that can be reduced immediately with zero infrastructure changes. Parts delays, walking time, and handoffs require operational changes. Documentation does not.

Repetitive by Nature

Production floors see the same 40+ repair types every single day. Every technician types the same cause-and-correction format from memory — introducing inconsistency and wasted effort on work that never changes.

Compounds Across the Team

At 8 stories per day, 5 minutes each — a 10-person team loses over 400 minutes of productive capacity every single shift to documentation alone.

Invisible on Dashboards

Story time doesn't clock in as a separate metric. It hides inside job completion time and end-of-shift idle time, making it easy to overlook in standard reporting.

Quality Suffers Too

Rushed documentation leads to incomplete stories, spelling errors, and missing steps — creating downstream problems for warranty claims, audits, and production history.

Primary Impact

Production Department

Production sees the highest volume of repeated work — the same vehicle, the same defects, the same stories written dozens of times a day. This is where the time loss is greatest and where autosuggest delivers the most immediate impact. Every technician on the production floor is a candidate for immediate productivity gains.

Highest Volume Most Repetitive Greatest ROI
Also Benefits

Service Department

While service handles a broader range of repairs, a significant portion of service work overlaps with production — the same common defects, the same documentation requirements. Service technicians face identical documentation friction and will benefit from the same templates without any additional setup or cost.
